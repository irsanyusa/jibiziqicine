As you may know, last season’s winning Dancing with the Stars pro Daniella Karagach is married to fellow pro dancer Pasha Pashkov. Recently, Karagach shared a funny blooper from their rehearsal for the DWTS tour, as the couple celebrates 13 years dancing together.

Daniella Karagach Shares Blooper with Husband Pasha Pashkov

In the clip posted to Karagach’s Instagram, the couple dances a sexy routine to “Crazy in Love” by Beyoncé. At one point, Pashkov runs toward Karagach and grabs her. The two of them end up falling over on the floor.

In her caption, Karagach posted plenty of cry-laughing emojis, calling it her “favorite blooper of all time” and adding in a hashtag that her “left butt cheek was purple.” Pashkov commented on the clip, writing, “I love how the lyrics right before that say ‘you got me, you got me.'”

Earlier this week, Karagach shared another Instagram post celebrating the 13th anniversary of her and Pashkov becoming dance partners, writing, “You are my person @pashapashkov and I couldn’t imagine sharing our laughs, tears, victories, losses, spontaneous adventures, griefs, competitions, and experiences with anyone else on this planet.”

The Couple’s Relationship Timeline

The couple first started dancing together in 2009, when Karagach was in her teens and Pashkov was in his 20s. “My coaches warned me that because I was so young and he was a top dancer in the finals already, people might not respect us as a compatible pair,” Karagach has said of their team-up.

The partnership worked out, as the pair competed in dance competitions around the world and even won at the Blackpool Dance Festival in 2017. They also became a couple, tying the knot in 2014. In 2018, they appeared together on Season 2 of World of Dance before joining Dancing with the Stars in 2019.

“It is funny, because my mom, just before [we tried out together], was asking: ‘What if you start liking him. What if you start having feelings for him?'” Karagach recalled, according to Distractify. “And I was like, ‘No way, he is so old! It’s never going to happen.'”

The couple told Entertainment Tonight that they want to have kids eventually, but they’re focused on their careers for now. “I want a baby so bad,” Karagach shared. “But for now, our bodies are our tools. This is our priority.”
