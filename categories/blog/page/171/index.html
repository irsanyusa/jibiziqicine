Merry Christmas or Happy Holidays? Most People Don't Care

published on July 14, 2024
Most people, regardless of their religious affiliation, aren’t bothered by companies explicitly acknowledging the Christmas holiday or businesses that stay neutral, according to a recent Morning Consult poll. Sixty-six percent of U.S. adults polled said it’s appropriate for store employees to say “Merry Christmas” to customers, compared to 8 percent who think it’s inappropriate. That’s similar to the 61 percent who said it’s appropriate for staff to wish shoppers “Happy Holidays,” while 13 percent said such well wishes are inappropriate. Read More...
