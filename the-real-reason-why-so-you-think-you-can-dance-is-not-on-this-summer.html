The Real Reason Why 'So You Think You Can Dance' is Not On This Summer

TALENTRECAP.COM Talent So You Think You Can Dance

Photo by: Ray Mickshaw/FOX

Reading Time: 2 minutes

In a surprising turn of events, FOX has dropped a bombshell on dance enthusiasts by excluding the beloved reality competition show, So You Think You Can Dance, from its summer 2023 schedule. The absence of this dance extravaganza has left fans wondering about the fate of their favorite show. Speculations about its cancellation have been circulating, but what is the real reason behind this decision?

Why SYTYCD Will Not Air This Summer

FOX recently announced its summer lineup, unveiling a slate of original programming that includes popular shows like Crime Scene Kitchen, Beat Shazam, Don’t Forget The Lyrics, Gordon Ramsay’s Food Stars, and MasterChef. However, noticeably missing from the schedule is the iconic dance competition that has captivated audiences for years.

One might wonder why FOX would sideline a show that has consistently delivered entertainment, showcased incredible talent, and garnered a dedicated fan base. The answer may lie in a combination of factors that have contributed to the network’s decision.

First and foremost, the ratings of the previous season were not as stellar as expected. Despite a devoted following, SYTYCD struggled to attract a broader audience, resulting in lower viewership numbers.

Another significant factor that may have swayed the network’s choice is the unfortunate passing of one of the show’s beloved judges, Stephen “tWitch” Boss. As a prominent figure in the world of dance and entertainment, tWitch’s absence has undoubtedly left a void in the hearts of both fans and the production team.

Additionally, the financial aspect cannot be overlooked. Last season, FOX opted to produce the show on a more cost-effective scale by eliminating live episodes. This decision, although necessary for budgetary reasons, could have affected the overall production value and viewer engagement.

The Show May Still Air in The Future

It is however important to note that this absence from the summer schedule does not necessarily mark the end of the dance extravaganza. FOX has left the door open for “additional summer programming” to be announced, potentially indicating a future return for the show.

In the meantime, dance enthusiasts will have to find alternative ways to fulfill their passion for movement and creativity. It’s also a chance for aspiring dancers to focus on honing their skills and preparing for future opportunities, be it on SYTYCD or other platforms.

While the absence of SYTYCD from the summer schedule may disappoint fans, it’s essential to remember that change often brings new possibilities. The decision made by FOX to exclude the show this season might be the catalyst for a fresh and exciting chapter in the series.
